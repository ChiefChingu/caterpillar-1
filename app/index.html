Every year thousands of people report injuries caused by the oak processionary. This Spring promises to show an explosion of caterpillars due to the soft winter.

Climate change makes Northwestern Europe an increasingly attractive place for the oak processionary. Unfortunately, it is here to stay.

Luckily, being prepared can prevent a lot of problems.

Know where to go and where to stay away! Wouldn't it be great if you can spot (potential) areas that are not safe to go to?

Together we can map where oaks are infected and where not. So, before you engage in any outdoor activities, you can look for safe spots. Safe spots to let your children play. Safe spots to walk your dog. Safe spots to do your daily routines like commuting, doing groceries, etc.

What are the symptoms of contact?

Add an alert by tapping on the map or using the + button. You can add infected trees and healthy trees.

If you alerted the community services you can also specify this in your map entry.

If you see an alert, but do not see the infection, you can flag that alert as possibly incorrect. If multiple people do this, the alert will be removed.
